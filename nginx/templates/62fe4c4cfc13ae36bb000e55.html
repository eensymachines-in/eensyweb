Types of metering

There are 4 principles in gas metering

  • Mass flow measurement
  • Velocity flow measurement
  • Differential pressure
  • Positive displacement meters

They are based on 3 characteristics of fluid

  • Positive displacement meters collect a fixed fluid volume, then release and refill the gas or liquid. Then, tally how many times the capacity fills to determine the flow.
  • When measuring the rate of fluid over a known area, one can determine the flow.
  • Other methods depend on the flowing streamâ€™s forces as it overpowers a known constriction and indirectly calculates flow.

The most common ones found in my country are the PD meters, hence we would be dwelling into details of the same. Positive displacement meters require fluid to displace components mechanically and measure volumetric flow at the operating temperature and pressure. While they have sufficient accuracy, pressure and temperature compensation are needed to achieve mass flow, and since they have moving parts, the user must consider gas cleanliness. A PD meter may be called a PD flow meter or a volumetric flow meter.

Current billing process

Majority of the service providers have installed the mechanical diaphragm meter with analogue reading panel. The meters are typically located inside the consumer's premises such that the service provider has no means of accessing and verifying the reading. With montly billing cycles this is a repeated labor intensive task that demands dedicated manpower.

  1. Each month the service provider will send out reminders to submit legible snapshots of the meters.
  2. Deligent customers will send the snapshots denoting the current meter reading.
  3. The service provider then employs a workforce which can track, map and digitally convert the current reading.
  4. Billing is then calculated on the then unit prices. - unit prices vary with government directions.
  5. Billing amounts in INR are then sent to each of the customer.
  6. The team employed by the service provider then painstakingly follows up with each customer for payment of bills.
  7. Defaulters are then intimated about the late payment surcharges and even possible discontinuity of services.
  8. Bill adjustments and roll overs, gas leak surcharges are then also appended to the next billing cycle.
  9. Consumers who pay bills on/before time are then granted waivers and offers.

As you can notice the process is far from being lean, the customer gets overwhelmed with reminders and manual intensive process of reading conversion is prone to a lot of errors. This isn't really economical for the service provider in the longer run. We need some IoT intervention and cloud assistance to get this straight.

Enter IoT and cloud

Electronic camera module with a motherboard, mounted near the meter can take snapshots of the reading panel. This can be programmed to be done at the billing date. The motherboard can then hop onto the WiFi available nearby and post the photograph to a secured database on the cloud. OCR (Optical character recognition) algorithm can make digital values from the photographs and since each electronic unit is associated with one consumer, this can be regarded as valid current month's reading. Billing can then be instantly calculated for the consumer-month and invoice is sent over a messaging application/email. Digital invoices have instant pay options and once the consumer has paid the bill, service provider's database is updated.

Notice how since the electronics on site can do major heavy lifting of clicking punctual, legible photographs of the panel, the manual effort of following-up with the consumer unecessary. This leads to consumer convenience and hence better relations. Incase of changes in unit rate, or leakage surcharges the service provider now can keep all the consumers updated well before the billing is generated.

Defaulters can be tracked and notified of the late payment surcharges, with a software algorithm calculations become instant and accurate.

But you must also know..

  1. Since the electronic unit taking photographs is located inside the consumer's premises, its their responsibility to maintain it. Any deliberate external damage/alteration is attributed/chargeable to them
  2. Accordance to government guidelines no electrical power supply can be in 3ft vicinity of the gas pipeline
  3. This system works on the presumption that Home WiFi is always available for connection. 4G modules can be provided incase WiFi is not enabled, but this will add signifcant cost to the solution.
  4. Limited local storage is available incase the device has to go offline. Incase the device is offline for more than a stipulated time, manual intervention is required from the service provider.
