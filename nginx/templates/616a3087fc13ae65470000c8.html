Traditional home automation systems were designed keeping the mind limited connectivity. Not that automation was not prevalent, but it had to have you there to set/alter the configuration. So what you could have done standing beside a switch was what you would do to an automation. Notice how automation wasn't a stand out. But with emergence of cloud you can now be anywhere on the face of the planet, connect to internet and send commands back to your nifty automation device at home. Its this remote connectivity that makes Internet-of-things so unique and relevant.

Ever since internet became available to the public in 1991, various features were unravelled across the globe. Technology has evolved in a futuristic way making our lives far simpler. Smart home devices help ease decision-making, solve daily challenges, and make life healthier and safer. You, as a house owner need no longer worry about if a certain switch has been turned OFF or ON on time, Or wish there was someone who could help you with the switches when on vacation. Cloud coupled with automation can now allow you to control/set/configure remotely from your handheld.

Connectivity is a new default

Lack of connectivity sets us back to the stone age. The devices we propose would be just automation devices if not connected to the internet. Quite similar to a smart fone without a data connection! Lack of connectivity is one of the major problems that influence user experience and hampers adoption. WiFi is a necessity and no longer a luxury for the neo-urban homes. We can hence see home automation devices using this pattern-shift to the new default as a base on which it can thrive. Personalised connectivity hubs like Zigbee, Z-Wave, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth and other IoT connectivity technologies will take over the smart home market in 2021.

Infinite field remote

Cloud enablement brings us an array of possibilities that would have been fiction a decade back. Devices on the ground hook onto the local WiFi to go online. While users using their handhelds push commands & setttings through mobile web applications. This arrangement makes it possible for the user to roam around anywhere in the ambit of the internet signal field and send commands back home where the device are tuned in with anticipation. Blue tooth field-range limitation and physical tough screen buttons /peripherals are grossly frustrating. If you were ok with being 'within field' of the device to have to change its setting you might as well go back tp operating switches like you did when there wasnt any automation at all, wouldn't we?

Sentient homes

Sentient homes symbolizes the idea of future homes, homes that are a much better / convenient places to dwell in (I have a feeling they would be much more fun of place too). Such homes would have inmate preferences on one side while the external/internal physical conditions on the other. Intuitively the homes could negotiate the best possible settings/time of operations for your appliances leaving you to worry much less and enjoy your home much more.

The home will automatically adjust in real-time, a spectrum of facilities that support individuals, preferences inefficiency and weather patterns. On a whole, these smart houses are totally automatic that the house routines on their own.

Consumer security, privacy and confidence

There is no denying the hesitancy that consumers have when anything involves cloud since it involves the risk of data breach, however it has not stopped anyone from expecting more. We at Eensymachines are transperant when it comes to letting the users know how their data is being used (if at all) further. In coming years and beyond, smart home manufacturers will concentrate more on how to collect minimum data and provide maximum service to consumers.
