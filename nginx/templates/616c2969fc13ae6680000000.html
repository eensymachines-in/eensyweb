Not that traditional switches were a bad idea, but they havent been able to keep up with the times. Inanimate and painfully reactive, after having used them for so long its about time they start understanding better, yes we mean: Switches ! Wouldn't it be better if they could store our preferences and remember to turn ON/OFF just when we need it ? Clocks and switches have been around since centuries, but we had to wait till the advent of internet to think about switches that could take our commands while on the move from anywhere. We have tried our hands at bluetooth automation but range was again a issue.

Switches as internet of things

Single board computers were a revolution, in that they allowed us to customize devices closer to user preferences. Relays when coupled with such devices can give a internet gateway and make them come alive. A cloud can then serve as a route to commmands from the user and data from the device (on ground).

Smart phones have got almost all of us online, all that remains is to get our things online. We @Eensymachines would recommend mobile-web over mobile applications but the central idea still is to achieve sending commmands from your handhelds to the remote devices.

Switches scheduled to a clock

If all that you could do different was a mobile app instead of switch, IoT would really be redundant. But we use the remote connectivity to let the user set the switch schedule once and then just let it run over and over again against a 24 hour clock. Customizations include distinct clocks for each of the switches and setting a blanket schedule for all the switches all at once

Areas of application?

Just about anywhere where you need a group of (or single) electrical switches being operated remotely and set against a clock. Now we can leave that to your imagination but a couple of popular examples are here

  • Residential societies: Porches of residential buildings are illuminated as per the mandates from General Body Meetings (GBM). Yet there are floors that have specific/exceptional preferences regarding time of ON/OFF. With Autolumin this is as easy as it gets. Blanket schedule for all switches, plus custom ones for each individual.
  • Aquarium accessories: Scheduling aquarium accessories to a clock can actually render a more comfortable environment for your pets. Overtime illumination can cause stress and foster algae growth. While short illumination can make your pets sedantary. Aquaminder can precisely control the environment, you can sit back and change the configurations from your handheld.
How do you use such switches?

These units replace and bypass the traditional switches entirely. A trained electrical professional can help balance the amperage and thus club the appliances to the available switches (nodes). We provide no failsafe within so recommend to use it serially ahead of an MCB / over current protector.
